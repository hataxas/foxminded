Steve Jobs

1955 – 2011

American entrepreneur, industrial designer, business magnate, media proprietor, and investor.

Steven Paul Jobs (February 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011) is widely recognized as a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 1970s and 1980s, along with his early business partner and fellow Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak.
Official Apple photo from 2011

The following list is a time line of Stevens Life

  • 1955 - Steven Paul Jobs was born in San Francisco, California, on February 24, 1955, to Joanne Carole Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali (Arabic: عبد الفتاح الجندلي). His cousin, Bassma Al Jandaly, maintains that his birth name was Abdul Lateef Jandali. He was adopted by Clara (née Hagopian) and Paul Reinhold Jobs.
  • 1959 - the family had moved to the Monta Loma neighborhood in Mountain View, California.
  • 1968 - He began his first year in Homestead High School with Bill Fernandez, who introduced Jobs to Steve Wozniak, and would become Apple's first employee.
  • 1972 - Jobs enrolled at Reed College in Portland, Oregon. After just one semester, Jobs dropped out of Reed College without telling his parents.
  • 1976 - Wozniak completed the basic design of the Apple I computer and showed it to Jobs, who suggested that they sell it. Wozniak was at first skeptical of the idea but later agreed.
  • 1977 - Jobs and Wozniak introduced the Apple II at the West Coast Computer Faire.
  • 1978 - at age 23, Jobs was worth over $1 million (equivalent to $4.15 million in 2021). By age 25, his net worth grew to an estimated $250 million (equivalent to $745 million in 2021). He was also one of the youngest "people ever to make the Forbes list of the nation's richest people—and one of only a handful to have done it themselves, without inherited wealth".
  • 2003 - Jobs was diagnosed with cancer.
  • 2011 - Jobs died at his Palo Alto, California, home.
